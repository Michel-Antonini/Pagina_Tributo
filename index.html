Linus Benedict Torvalds

One of the world's most influential computer scientists!!

Here's a time line of Linus Torvalds life:

  • 1969 - Born in Helsinki, Finland, on December 28 of that year.
  • 1980 - He began his computer adventures at the age of 11.
  • 1988 - He was admitted to the University of Helsinki, where he studied computer science.
  • 1989 - Joined the Nyland Brigade of the Finnish Navy, selecting the 11-month officer training program to fulfill Finland's compulsory military service.
  • 1990 - He started learning the C programming language at his university.
  • 1996 - An asteroid was named 9793 Torvalds in honor of Linus Torvalds.
  • 1997 - Received the 1997 Nokia Foundation Award from Nokia and the Lifetime Achievement Award at Uniforum Pictures.
  • 2005 - Created Git, a version control software, with the efficiency and reliability of application versioning in mind when applications have a large number of source code files.
  • 2006 - Business 2.0 magazine named him "one of the ten people who don't matter" because the growth and importance of Linux had overshadowed Linus' individual impact.
    Time magazine named him one of Linux's revolutionary heroes. the last 60 years.
  • 2008 - He was inducted as a Fellow at the Computer History Museum in Mountain View, California "for creating the Linux kernel and managing the open source development of the widely used Linux operating system".
  • 2010 - Received the NEC Corporation C&C Award for "contributions to the advancement of the information technology industry, education, research, and the improvement of our lives".
  • 2012 - The Finnish Academy of Technology awards him the 2012 Millennium Technology Prize (which he shares with Dr Yamanaka) "in recognition of his creation of a new open source operating system for computers with a Linux kernel."
    In the Internet Society Global INET conference in Geneva, Switzerland, Torvalds inducted into Internet Hall of Fame, one of ten in the Innovators category.
  • 2014 - The Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers awards him the IEEE Computer Society's Computer Pioneer Award.
    The Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers awards him the IEEE Masaru Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award.
  • 2022 - On December 28 of that year, Linus Torvalds turns 53 years old.

Famous Phrase:

"Talk is cheap. Show me the code."

This sentence was Linus' reply in a heated argument with Jamie Lokier.
